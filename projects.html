Associated with University of Maryland – Robert H. Smith School of Business
Aug 2025 – Aug 2025
  • Yield Curve Construction: Built initial term structures by calibrating Nelson–Siegel, Nelson–Siegel–Svensson, cubic spline, and linear interpolation models to market yield data.
  • Model Implementation: Developed and implemented Vasicek and Cox–Ingersoll–Ross (CIR) stochastic interest rate models to capture mean‑reverting dynamics, reflecting realistic economic and market conditions.
  • Bond & Derivative Pricing Framework: Created a pricing framework using the developed models to value fixed income securities, caps, floors, and bonds with embedded options, incorporating realistic interest rate movements.

  • Sensitivity and Scenario Analysis: Conducted sensitivity and scenario testing to evaluate the impact of various interest rate paths on valuations, informing risk/return characteristics of fixed‑income portfolios.

Credit Risk Modelling using PD, LGD and EAD models with Machine Learning Techniques
Associated with University of Maryland – Robert H. Smith School of Business
Apr 2025 – May 2025
  • Developed an end‑to‑end credit risk pipeline using LendingClub data (2007–2014) to estimate PD, LGD, EAD, and combined Expected Loss (EL).
  • Analyzed and processed variables (loan status, DTI, employment length, home ownership); applied Weight of Evidence (WoE) and Information Value (IV) to encode categorical features for interpretability.
  • Validated PD, LGD, and EAD models in line with Basel III IRB standards, aligning with regulatory capital frameworks.
  • Built a logistic‑regression PD model with statistically significant variables; achieved AUROC 0.72, Gini 0.44, KS 0.3208 on the test set.
  • Created a credit scorecard; analyzed thresholds via TPR/FPR; monitored PD model with Population Stability Index (PSI) for drift detection.
  • Implemented a two‑stage LGD model: Stage 1 logistic regression to classify recovery (AUROC 64.8%), Stage 2 linear regression on recovered accounts (recovery rate > 0) achieving RMSE 0.09.
  • Modeled EAD using linear regression on CCF‑based features; actual vs predicted correlation 53.06%.
  • Computed EL at account and portfolio levels; total expected loss $426.9M over $6.66B funded exposure (EL rate 6.41%).
